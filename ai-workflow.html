Running a program (almost) solo, on one roster and an AI layer

A workflow I designed, and run, where AI genuinely delivered — the same evaluate, build, and adopt motion I would bring to a practice group, done out of necessity on my own operation. For several years I have helped run a guaranteed-admission undergraduate honors program that prepares students for medical school, in partnership with a college of medicine. When budget pressure cut the office from four people to essentially one, I kept the whole pipeline running by building an AI layer over a single system of record. I stayed because I believe in the mission; I rebuilt the operation so it could survive on a fraction of the staff.

The architecture

Many messy sources, one source of truth

The whole system rests on one idea: every data source flows through a secure AI layer into a single master roster, and every output is generated back out of it. The roster is the repository; the AI is the connective tissue that keeps it current and turns it into action.

The work

Three data-heavy workflows, one operator

The office owns two functional pipelines plus the cohort management that connects them. Each was a spreadsheet-and-email nightmare before; each now runs on the same AI-over-roster pattern.

1 Admissions — 900 applications to a cohort of 30

Applications arrive in Slate and get bulk-downloaded. I use the university’s enterprise-grade Microsoft Copilot to securely sift that data and build the tracking spreadsheets the review team works from — just the director, the associate director, and me. Copilot connects the pieces: pull from Slate, normalize into Excel, organize the email threads, and schedule the 100-plus interviews that narrow the pool to 30. My first cycle, before AI, was a nightmare of manual reconciliation; now the data moves itself, and the humans spend their time on the judgment that actually matters — who to admit.

2 Cohort management & communications

Once admitted, students become a cohort to coordinate: roster upkeep, listserv communications, class scheduling and room booking, and a calendar of program events — orientation, town halls, lectures, capstone presentations, recruiting sessions, and graduation. I track all of it through email with the honors college and the university, with responses captured in Qualtrics, documents in Box, and everything reconciled back to the master roster. AI drafts and personalizes the mass communications (mail-merge from Word), summarizes response data, and keeps the roster — the repository everything is pulled from — current.

3 The medical-school transition

Our office also facilitates the hand-off into medical school. I draft letters from the director on students’ behalf, work inside their application portals, support med-school prep, and maintain tracking documents through Blackboard and now Canvas. AI accelerates the drafting and the tracking; a human writes the judgment and signs the letter. It is the same “AI drafts, the person decides” discipline that runs through my legal work.

The keystone

The master roster as the system of record

None of this works without one decision: a single master roster as the source of truth. Every applicant, every cohort member, every status and deadline lives there. The AI’s job is to keep that one sheet current from many sources and to generate everything — trackers, schedules, communications, letters — back out of it. That is what stops a one-person operation from drowning in version conflicts: you don’t chase data across ten systems, you maintain one repository and let the AI move it.

The guardrails

Why this is safe to run on AI

01

Enterprise, not consumer

Applicant and student records are sensitive (FERPA). I use the university’s enterprise-grade, secured AI — never a public chatbot — for anything touching real data.

02

Human owns the judgment

AI sifts, drafts, and reconciles. People decide who is admitted and what a director’s letter says. The high-stakes calls never automate.

03

One source of truth

A single master roster means changes are reviewable and traceable — no shadow spreadsheets drifting out of sync.

04

Built to be handed off

I documented the processes and a knowledge base so my successor inherits a system, not a mystery — the work outlasts me.
